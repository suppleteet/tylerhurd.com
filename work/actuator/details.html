Overview

Actuator is a tool I built that leverages real-time physics and tracked VR devices for rigging, animating and puppeteering to give you fast and convincing results.

Over the course of Chorus and Beach Body Bros I incorporatied real-time physics for puppeteering and animation and the results are most excellent! In VR you're trying to convince the viewer of an object's weight and movement, and a real-time physics simulation will give you far more convincing results than hand-keyed animation alone. Not only that it gives you the ability to collide and interact with otherwise static lifeless characters. I believe this technique of harnessing real-time physics using 6DOF devices is where motion authoring is headed, and during development I noticed there aren't any tools to aid in this process, that's where the idea for Actuator came from.

The goal of Actuator is to leverage real-time physics and tracked devices into the entire character performance pipeline. Start with a 3D model, rig it by drawing physics primitives, and then set up a physically based puppet or create animation curves using the real-time physics as the secondary/follow-through.
